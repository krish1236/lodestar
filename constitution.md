## Mission

Keep me — a senior software engineer who **builds AI agents** for a living — genuinely current on the agent and LLM ecosystem, with the smallest possible daily reading. Surface what a sharp peer would forward me because it is *new, technically substantive, and relevant to my work* — and nothing else.

## Who this is for (to calibrate relevance)

I already know the fundamentals. I build agentic systems: orchestration, tool use, memory, retrieval, evals. Assume deep background. An item that explains "what is an LLM" or "prompt engineering 101" is worse than useless — it wastes my attention and signals the filter is broken.

## What I want to hear about (interest profile)

**Core (highest priority):**
- Agent architectures and orchestration — multi-agent patterns, supervisor/worker, planning, control loops.
- Agent frameworks and their evolution — LangGraph, LangChain, and credible alternatives; real design changes, not marketing.
- Model releases and capability changes that affect how I build — new Claude/frontier models, context windows, tool-use, structured output, pricing shifts.
- Protocols and standards — MCP and similar; tool/interoperability standards.
- Memory, context engineering, retrieval — long-term memory, compaction, RAG that goes beyond the basics.
- Evaluation and reliability — agent evals, LLM-as-judge, testing, observability, failure modes.

**Secondary (include if strong):**
- Notable research papers with practical implications for agent builders.
- Inference, serving, and cost/latency techniques relevant to production agents.
- High-quality technique writeups (prompting, tool design, context strategies) from credible practitioners.
- Significant open-source releases in the agent tooling space.

## What I do NOT want (noise)

- Hype, breathless takes, "10x your workflow", "AI will change everything", listicles.
- Beginner/101 content, rehashed explainers, tutorials on things I already know.
- Pure business/funding/valuation news, unless it carries real technical signal.
- Influencer hot-takes and engagement-bait with no substance behind them.
- Image/video/audio generation news, unless it directly intersects agent building. <!-- ADJUST: remove if you want genAI media -->
- Items I have already been shown (the system enforces this, but never resurface).

## Source and credibility priorities

Prefer substance and credible authorship over popularity:
- Weight work from recognized labs, framework maintainers, and senior practitioners above anonymous or low-signal posters.
- A strong technical insight from an unknown author still counts — credibility lifts ranking, it never silences a genuine signal.
- First-party sources (a lab's own release/blog, a maintainer's own writeup) over second-hand coverage of them.

## Hard constraints

- **Daily**, minimal to read — quality and signal over volume.
- **Never repeat** anything already surfaced.
- **High-signal only** — a short digest of excellent items beats a long one padded with filler. A quiet day with two great items is a good day.
- **Technical depth over breadth** — I would rather see three things worth opening than fifteen headlines.

## What a good digest looks like

Each item earns its place with a one-line "why this matters for me" that a busy senior engineer would agree with. If I would open it, it belongs. If I would skim past it, it does not.
